HetroD Dataset

Dense, culturally grounded scooter–car–pedestrian interactions for autonomous‑driving research

Abstract

We present HetroD, a dataset and benchmark for developing autonomous‑driving systems in dense, heterogeneous environments. Unlike prior datasets focused on lane‑disciplined traffic, HetroD captures culturally grounded behaviors such as hook turns, lane splitting, and informal right‑of‑way negotiation. It comprises 200 k agent trajectories—including cars, scooters, buses, cyclists, and pedestrians—with centimetre‑accurate annotations, HD maps, traffic‑signal states and a modular tool‑chain for extracting per‑agent scenarios. HetroD enables modeling behaviors of vulnerable road users (VRUs) and constructs standardized benchmarks for forecasting, planning, simulation and multi‑agent behaviour modeling. Preliminary results show that state‑of‑the‑art models trained on existing datasets struggle to generalize, revealing key limitations in handling heterogeneous traffic.
